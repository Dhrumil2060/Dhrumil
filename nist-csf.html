NIST-CSF Strategy Implementation

Overview: This project presents the cybersecurity framework implementation for TrueNorth Construction Inc. using the NIST CSF 2.0, focused on risk management, governance, and incident response aligned with industry best practices.

1. Cybersecurity Needs

  • Protection of client/project data and financial records
  • Prevention of phishing, ransomware, and insider threats
  • Mobile device security and remote access controls
  • Vendor compliance and security awareness training

2. Current Practices & Goals

Includes antivirus and firewall protections, employee training, and manual backups with goals to enhance infrastructure, centralize management, and implement incident response plans.

3. NIST CSF 2.0 Adoption Benefits

  • Improved risk management and governance
  • Regulatory compliance alignment
  • Enhanced threat resilience
  • Building stakeholder trust

4. Key Functions of the Framework

  • Identify: Asset management and risk assessment
  • Protect: Access controls and data security
  • Detect: Security monitoring and anomaly detection
  • Respond: Incident response planning
  • Recover: Recovery strategies

5. Access Control & MFA Strategies

Role-based access control (RBAC), attribute-based access control (ABAC), and multi-factor authentication (MFA) methods implemented for granular, context-aware security.

6. Encryption & Data Handling

Use of AES-256, BitLocker, OpenSSL, and GPG for protecting data at rest and in transit, alongside strict data classification and backup procedures.

7. Security Awareness & Training

Comprehensive training plans including phishing simulations and ongoing employee education to foster a strong security culture.
